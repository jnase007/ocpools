Pool Care Guide

Tips from our team to keep your pool in perfect shape between service visits.

Between Visits

What You Can Do at Home

Daily Visual Check

  • Glance at water clarity — it should be blue, not cloudy or green
  • Check the water level — it should hit the middle of the skimmer opening
  • Remove any large debris (leaves, branches) with a leaf net if you see them

Run Your Equipment

  • Keep your pump running 8–12 hours daily (we'll set the optimal schedule)
  • Never turn off the pump for more than 24 hours in warm weather
  • If you hear unusual grinding or humming, call us before it gets worse

After Heavy Use

  • Run the pump for an extra 2–3 hours after a pool party
  • Shower before swimming to reduce sunscreen and oil buildup
  • Text us if you're hosting more than 6 guests — we can pre-treat

After Rain or Wind

  • Empty skimmer baskets if they're full of leaves and debris
  • Don't add chemicals yourself — rain changes pH and we'll rebalance
  • If water level rises above the tile, let us know so we can drain to optimal level

Know Your Water

Chemical Balance Basics

You don't need to be a chemist — that's our job. But understanding what we measure helps you spot issues early.

pH Level

7.4 ideal

Range: 7.2 – 7.6. Low pH burns eyes. High pH clouds water and reduces chlorine effectiveness.

Below 7.2

  • • Eye & skin irritation
  • • Corrodes metal equipment
  • • Etches plaster surfaces
  • Fix: Add 6–8 oz soda ash per 10K gal (raises pH ~0.2)

Above 7.6

  • • Cloudy water
  • • Chlorine becomes inactive
  • • Scale buildup begins
  • Fix: Add 8–12 oz dry acid or ¾ cup muriatic acid per 10K gal

Free Chlorine

3.0 ppm ideal

Range: 2 – 4 ppm. This is what kills bacteria and algae. Below 1 ppm, your pool is at risk.

Below 2 ppm

  • • Algae growth risk
  • • Bacteria thrive
  • • Green or murky water
  • Fix: Shock with 1 lb granular chlorine or 1 gal liquid chlorine per 10K gal

Above 4 ppm

  • • Skin & eye irritation
  • • Bleaches swimwear
  • • Strong chemical smell
  • Fix: Stop adding chlorine, run pump, wait 24–48 hrs. Sunlight helps burn it off

Total Alkalinity

100 ppm ideal

Range: 80 – 120 ppm. Acts as a buffer for pH. Without it, pH swings wildly.

Below 80 ppm

  • • pH bounces constantly
  • • Corrosion risk
  • • Green-tinted water
  • Fix: Add 1.5 lbs baking soda per 10K gal (raises TA ~10 ppm)

Above 120 ppm

  • • Cloudy water
  • • Scale deposits
  • • Chlorine less effective
  • Fix: Add 1 qt muriatic acid per 10K gal (lowers TA ~10 ppm). Don’t exceed 1 qt at a time

Calcium Hardness

300 ppm ideal

Range: 200 – 400 ppm. Too low corrodes surfaces. Too high causes white scale deposits.

Below 200 ppm

  • • Etches plaster & tile
  • • Corrodes equipment
  • • Pitting on surfaces
  • Fix: Add 1.25 lbs calcium chloride per 10K gal (raises CH ~10 ppm)

Above 400 ppm

  • • White scale on tile
  • • Cloudy water
  • • Clogs filters & pipes
  • Fix: Partial drain & refill (replace 1/4 to 1/3 of water). No chemical lowers calcium

Not sure where your levels stand? We test everything on every visit.

Year-Round Care

Seasonal Tips for SoCal Pools

Summer (Jun–Sep)

Peak season. Higher temperatures burn through chlorine faster. UV breaks down stabilizer. More swimmers means more demand on filtration.

  • • Run pump 10–12 hrs daily
  • • We increase chlorine and stabilizer levels
  • • Keep landscaping trimmed back from the pool

Fall (Oct–Nov)

Leaves are the biggest challenge. Decaying organic matter consumes chlorine and can stain surfaces if left too long.

  • • We add phosphate remover to prevent algae
  • • Consider a pool cover if you have heavy tree canopy
  • • Great time for filter deep-cleaning

Winter (Dec–Feb)

In Southern California, pools don't freeze — but they still need care. Reduced sunlight and cooler water mean less chlorine demand, but algae can still form in mild weeks.

  • • We reduce pump runtime to 6–8 hrs daily
  • • Ideal time for equipment upgrades or tile repair
  • • Keep the pool clean — don't "hibernate" it

Spring (Mar–May)

Pollen and Santa Ana winds bring debris. Water temps start climbing. This is when we ramp up and prep your pool for summer entertaining.

  • • We do a full equipment tune-up
  • • Acid wash or tile cleaning if needed
  • • Great time to schedule any resurfacing

Early Warning

Signs to Call Us Right Away

Most problems are easy to fix if caught early. Here's what to watch for.

Green or Cloudy Water

Algae growth or chemical imbalance. Don't swim — call us for same-day treatment.

Unusual Equipment Noise

Grinding, screeching, or loud humming from the pump or motor means bearings or seals may be failing.

White Scale on Tile

Calcium deposits from high hardness. Catches early, easy to clean. Left too long, requires acid wash.

Water Level Dropping Fast

Normal evaporation is ¼" per day. More than that could indicate a leak in plumbing or the shell.

Strong Chlorine Smell

Ironically, a strong "chlorine" smell means there isn't enough free chlorine. It's actually chloramines — waste byproducts.

Stinging Eyes After Swimming

pH is likely too low (acidic). Safe to exit, but don't swim again until we rebalance the water.
