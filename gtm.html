Phase 1

Hit the Doors in Newport Beach

High-end homes in Newport Beach, Newport Coast, and Corona del Mar are the bullseye. These homeowners invest $50K–$150K+ in their pools and expect premium service. Most are underserved — either with a mediocre service or doing it themselves badly.

1

Saturday & Sunday Canvassing

Homeowners are home on weekends. Walk the high-end streets — you can spot the pools from Google Maps before you go. Target homes with visible pools, outdoor entertaining setups, and signs of premium investment.

2

Scout from Google First

Use Google Maps satellite view to identify homes with pools. Build a hit list by street before the weekend. Prioritize cul-de-sacs and waterfront properties — they tend to have the largest pools and the highest budgets.

3

Test Their Water on the Spot

Bring the digital tester. Offer a free water analysis right at the door. It takes 60 seconds and gives you lab-quality data — chlorine, pH, alkalinity, calcium, CYA, metals. Nobody else is doing this. It immediately positions you as the pro.

4

Close on Value, Not Price

Show them the results. Explain what's off. Then pitch the remote monitoring model — "I'll install a monitor in your pool so I can watch it 24/7. You'll never have a green pool again, and I only come out when something actually needs attention." That's a conversation nobody else in the market is having.

The Digital Water Tester

LaMotte WaterLink Spin Touch (Model 3581) — a professional-grade photometer that reads 10+ water parameters in 60 seconds using disposable SpinDisk™ reagent pods.

This is what pool stores and labs use — except you're bringing it to their front door.

Phase 2

Remote Monitoring = Higher Margins

The traditional pool service model is broken: drive to every pool, every week, whether it needs you or not. The smart model: monitor remotely, visit only when there's an actual issue.

Traditional Model vs. OC Pools Model

Traditional Pool Service

  • Drive to every pool weekly — rain or shine
  • Revenue capped by drive time & routes
  • Most visits = nothing's actually wrong
  • Can't scale without hiring technicians

OC Pools Remote Model

  • Monitor every pool 24/7 from your phone
  • Only visit when an issue actually arises
  • Same monthly rate — way fewer truck rolls
  • Scale to 3–4x the client base with one person

The Pool Monitor

A smart WiFi-connected device (~$300) that sits in the client's pool and continuously monitors water chemistry — pH, ORP, temperature, and more. Data streams to your dashboard in real-time.

Recommendation

Either way, hook the smart monitor data into whichever app you pick. Client opens app → sees their pool is healthy → trusts you completely. That's the flywheel.

Execution

Launch Timeline

1

Week 1–2: Gear Up

Order the digital water tester (~$2,300) and a starter batch of test pods. Sign up for Skimmer or Paythepoolman. Order 5–10 smart monitors to have on hand.

2

Week 3–4: Scout & Canvass

Build your Newport Beach hit list from Google Maps satellite. Hit 20–30 doors per weekend. Offer free water tests. Target: 3–5 signed clients in the first month.

3

Month 2: Install & Monitor

Install smart monitors in every signed client's pool. Set up their profiles in the app. Start remote monitoring. Collect monthly payments.

4

Month 3+: Scale

Referrals kick in — Newport Beach homeowners talk. Continue weekend canvassing to expand. Target: 15–20 monitored pools by month 3. Add neighborhoods (Laguna, Dana Point). The app and monitors do the heavy lifting.
